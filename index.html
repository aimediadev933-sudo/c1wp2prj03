Workshop Overview

The SecureAsia Workshop aimed to strengthen the cybersecurity capabilities of network engineers and administrators across AsiaConnect partner countries. This three-day intensive training was jointly organized by Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM), Malaysian Research and Education Network (MYREN), and University of Hamburg (UH), Germany.

13 Countries

Training Activities

Day 1: Security Foundations

Covered physical security, malware, botnets, DDoS attacks, and social engineering through comprehensive lectures and live demonstrations.

Day 2: Detection & Defense

Linux/networking refresher followed by practical sessions on IDS, honeypots, sandboxes, and access control systems.

Day 3: Vulnerabilities & Hands-On

Participants engaged in vulnerability analysis, penetration testing, and competed in a Capture-the-Flag (CTF) challenge.

Additional Activities

  • Group photo sessions and certificate presentations
  • Official social dinner for informal networking
  • Creation of dedicated social media presence
  • Public-facing webpage for knowledge sharing

Key Outcomes

Improved Technical Skills

Participants learned to secure NREN infrastructure using tools like IDS, honeypots, firewalls, and ethical hacking techniques.

Enhanced Regional Collaboration

Created a strong network among cybersecurity professionals across 13 AsiaConnect countries.

Established Training Framework

Delivered a replicable cybersecurity training model combining theory, practice, and peer learning.
